INSTRUCTIONS

In this experiment, you will first listen to an incomplete sentence (e.g. just the beginning of a sentence). Then you will type an appropriate ending to the incomplete sentence you heard. All sentences will be about children playing with some toys like stuffed animals, craft supplies, clothing, etc. 

What are some example sentences?

The sentences will either be a description of a child's action (e.g. Jane colored the picture with her new...markers) or an adult's instruction (e.g. You can eat the cupcake after your...dinner). All of the sentences will also involve a stuffed animal (unlike the sentences above). 

For example: Mike loved to bring the moose to the...(movies? beach? backyard?). 

In the sentence above, the moose is a stuffed animal, so bringing him to the movies is a totally appropriate response. 

The other toys that children have to play with could be craft supplies (e.g. pipe cleaners, cotton balls, string, glitter, ) or common toys (e.g. bongos, kitchen sets, household objects ) Feel free to use your imagination! 

More examples:

Sentence: "You can walk the dog next to the..."   Ending: fire hydrant! 
Sentence: "You can annoy the elephant by throwing..."   Ending: pom-poms!
Sentence: "Anthony brought his dog with the brown..."  Ending: ears! 

Again, you will be listening to incomplete sentences and providing endings like the ones above. After providing your ending, you will be asked to retype the entire sentence (the sentence beginning + your new ending).

You will have as much time as needed to fully type in your answers!
